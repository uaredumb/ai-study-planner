# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Students under exam/deadline pressure who arrive with messy, unorganized notes and need them turned into something usable fast. They're often mid-cram: stressed, short on time, and looking for reassurance that the tool will make things clearer, not add more work. Secondary: students planning proactively across a term, and a broader self-learner audience — but the cramming-student moment is the one the interface should optimize for first.

## Product Purpose

AI Study Planner takes messy notes (pasted text) and turns them into clean notes, study tasks, and a suggested study order via an AI API (OpenRouter). Success looks like: a stressed student pastes notes, hits Clean Notes, and within seconds has a calmer, clearer picture of what to study and in what order — with minimal friction between "I have a mess" and "I have a plan." The product also has a Pro tier (code-gated) and a "God Mode" tier for elevated access.

## Brand Personality

Calm, clear, trustworthy. The interface should lower stress, not add to it — no urgency-manufacturing patterns, no clutter, no ambiguity about what to do next. Trustworthy extends to how AI output and Pro/paywall moments are presented: transparent, not manipulative.

## Anti-references

No specific named anti-references given. By inference from "calm, clear, trustworthy" and the cramming-student context: avoid aggressive SaaS-growth patterns (urgency countdowns, dark-pattern upsells, dense dashboard chrome) and avoid anything that reads as playful/gamified-first — gamification (XP, streaks) is a listed future feature but should stay secondary to clarity, not the dominant tone.

## Design Principles

1. **Calm over urgent.** Reduce visual noise and stress signals; no manufactured urgency in copy or motion, especially around Pro upsells.
2. **Clarity over cleverness.** The path from "paste messy notes" to "get a plan" should be obvious at a glance — this is the core job, and it must never compete with secondary features.
3. **Fast to reassurance.** Every state (loading, error, empty) should actively reduce anxiety, not just fill space — a stressed user reading a spinner needs to feel progress, not uncertainty.
4. **Trustworthy monetization.** Pro/God Mode gating and pricing should be transparent and honest, never coercive or confusing.
5. **Practice what you preach.** A tool that promises to bring order to chaos should itself feel ordered — clean hierarchy, no clutter, no orphaned UI.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA baseline: sufficient color contrast (light and dark themes already exist and must both meet 4.5:1 for body text), full keyboard navigation, and respect for `prefers-reduced-motion`. No additional needs specified beyond the AA baseline.
